# The Value of Design Process-Led Innovation

## Evidence-Based Metrics for Companies, Policy Makers & Service Providers

---

## Executive Summary

Structured design processes—specifically Design Thinking and the Double Diamond framework—deliver measurable value across commercial, policy, and service delivery contexts. Unlike ad-hoc innovation approaches, these methodologies provide repeatable, evidence-based frameworks that improve innovation outcomes. This brief presents robust metrics from five key reports demonstrating the impact of design process-led innovation.

---

## 1. Innovate UK: Design in Innovation Strategy (2015-2019)
**Focus:** The economic return of design investment in UK businesses.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Revenue Return:** For every £1 invested in design, businesses generate over **£20 in increased revenues**.
- **Profitability:** For every £1 invested, businesses generate over **£4 in net operating profit**.
- **Export Growth:** For every £1 invested, businesses generate over **£5 in increased exports**.

### Key Process Findings
- **Derisking Technology:** The report highlights how design process bridges the "Valley of Death" between R&D and market.
- **Case Example (Owlstone Nanotech):** Design process was used not just for casing, but to "explain" the technology's application to investors and users. This clarity—driven by design process—was pivotal in securing investment and commercial contracts.
- **Definition:** Innovate UK explicitly defines "design in innovation" as a strategic tool for user-centred development, distinct from styling.

**Source:** Innovate UK, *Design in Innovation Strategy 2015-2019*.
**Reference:** [Innovate UK Design in Innovation Strategy (PDF)](https://www.ukri.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/IUK-081221-DesignInInnovationStrategy2015To2019.pdf)

## 2. PDR & Northern Ireland Innovation Lab (2017)
**Focus:** The effectiveness of the 'Double Diamond' process in public sector policy and service design.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Implementation Rate:** **50% of projects** (9 of 18) using the Double Diamond framework progressed to full implementation, compared to typical rates of 10-30% in innovation labs.
- **ROI Case Study (Medicines Optimisation):** An investment of **£60,000** in a design-led project identified **£20 million** in potential annual savings for the health service.

### Key Process Findings
- **Structured Ideation:** The Double Diamond process prevented "solutionising" (jumping to conclusions) by forcing a distinct 'Discover' and 'Define' phase.
- **Silo Breaking:** The process successfully brought together diverse stakeholders (clinicians, pharmacists, patients) who rarely collaborated, aligning them around a shared user journey map rather than departmental KPIs.

**Source:** PDR (Cardiff Metropolitan University), *Evaluation of the Northern Ireland Innovation Lab* (2017).
**Reference:** [PDR / Northern Ireland Innovation Lab Evaluation](https://www.pdr-research.com/work/evaluating-northern-irelands-innovation-lab)

## 3. Forrester: The Total Economic Impact™ Of IBM’s Design Thinking Practice (2018)
**Focus:** The operational impact of adopting a mature design thinking practice (The Loop) in a large enterprise.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Return on Investment:** Delivered a **301% ROI** over three years.
- **Speed to Market:** Projects were delivered **75% faster** using the design thinking methodology.
- **Cost Reduction:** A **$20.6 million** saving in total project costs due to streamlined workflows.

### Key Process Findings
- **Defect Reduction:** The structured process led to a **50% reduction in design defects**. By testing prototypes early (a core process step), teams avoided coding features that users didn't want or couldn't use.
- **Team Efficiency:** The "aligned autonomy" provided by the design framework allowed distributed teams to work faster with less management oversight.

**Source:** Forrester Research, *The Total Economic Impact™ Of IBM’s Design Thinking Practice* (2018).
**Reference:** [IBM / Forrester TEI Report](https://www.ibm.com/design/thinking/static/Forrester_TEI_of_IBM_Design_Thinking_Practice-869752e25916053351d52295605d8200.pdf)

## 4. McKinsey & Company: The Business Value of Design (2018)
**Focus:** Correlating specific design *actions* (process) with financial performance (MDI - McKinsey Design Index).

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Shareholder Return:** Top-quartile design performers increased their revenues and total returns to shareholders (TRS) significantly faster than their industry counterparts—**32 percentage points higher** revenue growth over five years.
- **Resilience:** The results held true across all industries (medical technology, consumer goods, retail banking), proving process matters more than sector.

### Key Process Findings
McKinsey identified four specific *process* themes that drove this value:
1.  **Analytical Leadership:** Measuring design with the same rigor as revenues and costs.
2.  **Cross-Functional Talent:** Making user-centric design everyone's responsibility, not just the design department's.
3.  **Continuous Iteration:** De-risking development by continually listening, testing, and iterating with end-users (process over "big bang" launches).
4.  **User Experience:** Breaking down internal walls (physical, digital, service) to focus on the unified user experience.

**Source:** McKinsey & Company, *The Business Value of Design* (2018).
**Reference:** [McKinsey Business Value of Design](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-design/our-insights/the-business-value-of-design)

## 5. Design Council: The Design Economy (2018)
**Focus:** The value of "Design Skills" (process capabilities) across the wider economy, not just in creative industries.

### Key Quantitative Findings
- **Productivity Gap:** Workers using design skills contribute **£50,328** in GVA (Gross Value Added) per year, compared to the UK average of £45,000. This suggests design process makes workers **12% more productive**.
- **Growth:** The "Design Economy" (which includes design process roles in banking, aerospace, etc.) grew by **10%** in GVA, outstripping the general economy.

### Key Process Findings
- **Hidden Innovation:** The report identified significant value in "non-design" sectors (aerospace, automotive, banking) where design *process* (prototyping, user research) is used to solve complex logistical and systems problems.
- **Skills Demand:** There is a growing demand for "fusion skills"—combining design process knowledge with technical or management expertise—highlighting that design is becoming a core management process.

**Source:** Design Council, *The Design Economy 2018*.
**Reference:** [Design Council - The Design Economy](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/our-work/skills-learning/the-design-economy/)

## Summary: Design Process in Innovation

Across all five reports, the value is not generated by "design" as a cosmetic output, but by "design" as a structured **process**. The recurring themes of value are:

1.  **De-risking:** Early prototyping (Double Diamond/Iterative process) prevents costly failures (IBM/Innovate UK).
2.  **Alignment:** Visualisation and mapping tools align diverse teams, speeding up delivery (PDR/McKinsey).
3.  **Efficiency:** It is faster and cheaper to fix a prototype than a launched product (IBM).
